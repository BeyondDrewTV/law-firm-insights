# Clarion North Star

## What Clarion Is

Clarion is a law-firm client feedback governance platform. It turns uploaded client feedback into a partner-ready governance brief, a clear action record, and a repeatable review cycle.

It is not analytics software. It is not a dashboard product. It is not AI magic.
The product is valuable because it creates a disciplined governance loop that law firms can run repeatedly without building a reporting process around it.

---

## The Center of Gravity

**The governance brief is the product.**

Everything else — the signals view, the follow-through workspace, the upload flow, the PDF and email outputs — exists to produce and support that artifact. Every surface should feel like it belongs to the same system that generates the brief, not like a parallel set of features.

When in doubt about a design, UX, or copy decision: ask whether it makes the brief feel more central, or less.

---

## The Operative Loop

```
upload feedback → structure governance signals → assign actions → review progress → bring the brief into meetings → repeat
```

This loop is the product. It is not aspirational. It is what Clarion already does.

---

## Who This Is For

**Managing partners and operations leads at law firms** who need a disciplined client-feedback review cycle and currently have none — or have one that lives in scattered notes and meeting memory.

They are not data people. They are not looking for charts. They want to walk into a partner meeting with a clear record of what clients are saying, what the firm is doing about it, and what still needs a decision.

---

## What Clarion Should Feel Like

**Calm. Operational. Partner-ready.**

- A managing partner should be able to open the brief and immediately understand what matters without interpretation
- The workspace should feel like the operational system behind the brief — structured, not flashy
- The public site should feel like finding a trusted report, not a SaaS product tour
- PDF and email outputs should feel like the same artifact in a different transport mode — not like exports from a different product

**Not:** generic analytics platform, AI insight engine, dashboard for law firms, charts-first, flashy

---

## Canonical Output Hierarchy

1. **Governance brief** (on-screen ReportDetail, PDF, email) — the flagship artifact
2. **Follow-through record** (ExecutionPage) — downstream execution from the brief
3. **Signals view** (SignalsPage) — evidence layer feeding the brief
4. **Upload** — how a new cycle starts

Public proof mirrors this: sample brief first, sample workspace mechanics second.

---

## Canonical Brief Section Spine

```
1. Leadership Briefing
2. Signals That Matter Most
3. Assigned Follow-Through
4. Decisions & Next Steps
5. Supporting Client Evidence
```

This order is locked. All output surfaces (on-screen, PDF, email) use it.

## Design Direction (Persistent)

**Stay in this lane:**
- Calm, premium, editorial, document-forward, operational
- Serif display type (Newsreader) for brief artifacts and landing headlines
- Warm cream palette (`#F6F0E4`) for public surfaces; clean white with structured card layout for authenticated workspace
- Dark navy (`#0D1B2A`) as the primary ink and anchor color

**Motion:** restrained and purposeful — artifact reveals, subtle in-view transitions, tactile button feedback. No decorative animation.

**Avoid:** generic SaaS gradient spectacle, chart-first framing, floating UI clutter, "AI magic" visuals, noisy animation systems
